❓ Frequently Asked Questions

A biomarker is a biological molecule — such as a gene, protein, or mutation — that can help detect a medical condition, predict how it will progress, or show how well a treatment is working. In cancer, biomarkers help with diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment planning.
We use data from the Open Targets Platform, a public database that connects genes, diseases, and drugs. Our tool sends real-time queries to the Open Targets GraphQL API to get the most updated information.
We use AI (GPT-4) to generate concise, easy-to-understand and plain-language summaries for each biomarker. These summaries are based on scientific evidence from Open Targets and tailored to each specific disease.
The score (0 to 1) comes from Open Targets and reflects how strongly a biomarker is linked to a disease. A higher score means there's more evidence supporting that connection.
  • Predictive biomarkers help show if a treatment is likely to work.
  • Prognostic biomarkers give information about the likely course or outcome of a disease.
  • Diagnostic biomarkers help confirm the presence of a disease.
